Lets Ensure Safety

Trusted Adults

Trusted adults are people you can talk to if something makes you feel sad, scared, or uncomfortable.

Who are trusted adults? Parents, teachers, doctors, police officers, and grandparents.

SAFE/ UNSAFE Touch

Safe touch: A hug from someone you trust, like a family member or teacher

Unsafe touch: When someone touches you in a way that makes you feel uncomfortable or scared.

Body Safety Rules

My body belongs to me.

It's okay to say no to touches that make me feel uncomfortable.

I can always tell a trusted adult if something feels wrong.

Recognizing Uncomfortable Situations

Feeling scared or nervous around certain people.

Someone trying to touch you in a private area.

Or asking you to keep secrets from your parents or caregivers.

Emotional Awareness

Happy: Feeling joyful and safe.

Sad: Feeling upset or hurt.

Scared: Feeling afraid or worried.

Angry: Feeling mad or frustrated.

Confused: Feeling unsure or puzzled.

What to Do if You Feel Unsafe

Tell a trusted adult right away.

Remember, it's never your fault if someone makes you feel uncomfortable.
